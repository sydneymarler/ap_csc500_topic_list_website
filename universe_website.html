PLANET EARTH

The Earth is the only planet in the universe to harbor life (that we know of today). Earth contains about 70% water, the rest is terrestrial, but both regions are filled with life. Earth is approximately 4.5 billion years old. Earth is the third planet from the sun. Its tilted axis creates seasons. A year on Earth is approximately 365 days.

THE SOLAR SYSTEM

If an astronomical unit (a.u.) is the distance of the Earth to the sun, the end of the solar system is 125 a.u. away from the Earth. "The solar system consists of the Sun; the eight official planets, at least three 'dwarf planets', more than 130 satellites of the planets, a large number of small bodies (the comets and asteroids), and the interplanetary medium." -(nineplanets.org) There are potentially many more planetary satellites that have yet to be discovered.

LOCAL STAR CLUSTER

The nearest star to Earth is Alpha Centauri, a three-star system 4.367 light years away. With current technology, it would take over 100 years to reach. Anothe local star is a red giant, Betelguese, which is approximately 650 light years from Earth. At the end of its life, Betelguese is likely to become a supernova soon. The light, of course, would not reach the Earth until 650 years after the explosion.

THE MILKY WAY GALAXY

The local star cluster exists in just one arm of the Milky Way galaxy, far removed from the center, which is filled with dense star clusters. At the center of the Milky Way is a supermassive blackhole which gravitationally attracts stars and other celestial objects. The Milky Way is over 100,000 light years across.

LOCAL GROUP

Including the Milky Way, there are approximately 55 galaxies that make up the Local Group. The local group is over 10 million light years in diameter. (Wikipedia) The Milky Way has numerous satellite galaxies that orbit around it. The nearest supermassive galaxy to the Milky Way Galaxy is the Andromeda Galaxy. The two are set to eventually merge.
